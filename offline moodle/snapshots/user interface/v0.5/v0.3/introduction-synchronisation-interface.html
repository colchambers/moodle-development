Version 0.3: Synchronisation Interface

A demonstration version of the Offline Moodle Synchronisation Interface has been created to provide a clear indication of the functionality we intend to provide. The currnet version has been agreed with The Open University in principle and will be developed over the next coming versions with further functionality developed.

For the purposes of this demonstration we have focused on developing the following core functionality which will be available to all pages within the administration centre:
  • the intended look and feel.
  • the intended navigational elements.
  • examples of key pages and sections including; synchronisation home and management.
We believe that that there are two general types of screen the application will require:
  1. Screens to enter and manage information such as settings.
  2. Screens to view and administer information such as a management view.
With this release we have focused on providing examples of screens to manage the synchronisation process summarise and introduce the synchronisation feature. These screens will form the basis of all other screens where information is viewed and managed. We have not provided an example of how the settings or other screens will look, this will be provided in a future release.

Starting point

The demonstration begins in the Moodle Offline client viewing the home page.

Procedure

The demonstration begins with the Moodle home page and shows how the synchronisation feature will be reached. Access the synchronisation feature by clicking 'Synchronise Moodle' in the 'Synchronise' block on the right of the screen.

In this demonstration I would like to make some comments about the following areas:

Overall Synchronisation

Our first task is to develop the overall process of synchronisation. The key aspect of this has been developed in the page where Synchronisation itself is managed so most of our focus has gone there. These pages will form a template for the rest of the synchronisation process. We are currently working through each part of the section and defining how we will provide the required functionality.

The following list indicates the order in which we are currently developing the functionality and which areas are complete and ready for demonstration:
  1. Site home page
  2. Synchronisation
    1. Home page
    2. Configuration
    3. Synchronise
      1. Manage Synchronisation
      2. Display Results
  3. Course
    1. Home page
    2. Synchronisation Filtered
    3. Synchronised
  4. Forum
    1. Home page
    2. Synchronisation Filtered
    3. Synchronised

To date we have developed the functionality and look of the page to manage synchronisation. We have created a 'Manage Synchronisation' page which covers the processes of navigating content, performing synchronisation and resolving conflicts.

This page is the most fundamental part of the user experience and is why it has been provided first to provide plenty of time for feedback and future revisions.
